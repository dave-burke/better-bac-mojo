New Drink Help

This scene allows you to describe a drink you have consumed so that it can be incorporated into your BAC calculation.

The Alcohol By Volume field is the percentage of the drink that is pure alcohol. Enter a number only--do not include the % sign.

Alcohol By Volume is not the same as alcohol by weight.

ABV is 1/2 the "proof", so if you drink something that is "60 proof" then the ABV is 30%.

Some alcoholic beverages list this value on the packaging, but if they do not, you may have to estimate. You may also be able to find the ABV via a search of the internet. Many popular American pilsners are 4.3% ABV. Craft beers range from 4% to 10% abv, but probably average somewhere around 5%. Wine is usually about 12%, but can vary by a few percent. Dessert wines usually have a higher ABV, around 18%.

Calculating the ABV of a mixed drink can be difficult. One way to simplify is to ignore any non-alcoholic ingredients. For example, if you drink a Rum & Cola which consists of 2 oz. of 70 proof rum and 3 oz of cola, you could just enter "35" for the ABV and "2" for the volume.

The volume field should be ounces. A typical bottled beer is 12 oz. A 750ml bottle of Wine is about 25 oz. A glass of wine is usually between 3 and 6 oz. A standard "shot" is 1.5oz.

The time field may be used if you had a drink a while ago, but forgot to enter it at the time. You may only enter a time going back 24 hours. If you enter a "future" time (e.g. it is 7:00pm and you enter 8:00pm) then the drink will be interpreted as being from the previous day rather than later on in the current day. So in our example, that would be 8:00pm last night rather than 8:00pm later tonight.

I suspect that if you crossed time zones between drinks, it would screw up your calculations, but I have neither the means nor the ambition to test this.

You may use the "back" gesture to leave this scene and return to the main status screen without entering a new drink.
